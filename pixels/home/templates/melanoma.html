Melanoma Cancer

Symptoms of melanoma cancer can include changes in the appearance of moles or pigmented areas on the skin, such as asymmetry, irregular borders, variation in color, diameter greater than the size of a pencil eraser, or evolving size, shape, or texture. Other symptoms may include itching, bleeding, or ulceration of the affected area. Early detection through regular skin examinations and prompt medical evaluation of suspicious lesions is crucial for timely diagnosis and treatment.

Melanoma Cancer Treatment

Treatment for melanoma cancer depends on various factors including the stage of the cancer, its location, and the individual's overall health. Options may include surgical removal of the tumor, lymph node dissection, immunotherapy, targeted therapy, radiation therapy, or chemotherapy. Treatment plans are often personalized and may involve a combination of these approaches to effectively manage the cancer and prevent recurrence.
