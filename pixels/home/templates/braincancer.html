Brain Cancer Symptoms

Symptoms of brain cancer can vary depending on the type, location, and size of the tumor, but common symptoms may include headaches that worsen over time, seizures, difficulty with balance or walking, nausea or vomiting, changes in vision, weakness or numbness in the arms or legs, difficulty speaking or understanding speech, changes in personality or behavior, and cognitive impairment. It's important to note that these symptoms can also be caused by other conditions, so a thorough medical evaluation is necessary for accurate diagnosis and appropriate treatment.

Brain Cancer Treatment

Treatment for brain cancer depends on various factors including the type of tumor, its location, size, and grade, as well as the patient's overall health and preferences. Common treatment options may include surgery to remove the tumor, radiation therapy, chemotherapy, targeted therapy, immunotherapy, or a combination of these approaches. The goal of treatment is to remove or destroy the cancerous cells, alleviate symptoms, prevent recurrence, and improve quality of life. Treatment plans are personalized and may involve a multidisciplinary team of healthcare professionals. Close monitoring and follow-up care are essential components of managing brain cancer.
